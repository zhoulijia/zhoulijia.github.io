research


I am broadly interested in statistical learning theory. In particular, I have been thinking about problems related to high dimensional interpolation, benign overfitting, and implicit regularization.

High dimensional interpolation & benign overfitting

When the number of parameters in a prediction model is greater than the number of training samples, there usually exist infinitely many solutions that lead to zero training error (interpolation). In the context of high dimensional linear regression, we study the out-of-sample prediction error of the interpolator with the least Euclidean norm. More generally, we can consider ridge and LASSO regression as the amount of regularization goes to zero and other loss functions such as the squared hinge loss to include binary classification.

An interesting phenomena about these predictors is that they can sometimes achieve good out-of-sample performance despite overfitting to noisy observations. Moreover, contrary to the classical bias-variance trade-off, increasing the number of variables in the model can lead to better prediction accuracy. We develop a generalization theory to explain benign overfitting based on the technique of uniform convergence and we show that the norm of a predictor is the key to good generalization as the number of variables approaches infinity.

Understanding benign overfitting in generalized linear models is related to the theoretical study of deep neural networks because big interpolating networks also seem to generalize well in practice. This line of work is done as part of the Collaboration on the Theoretical Foundations of Deep Learning.

Implicit regularization of optimization algorithms

Since there are infinitely many solutions with zero training error in the over-parameterized regime, the choice of optimization algorithm plays a crucial role in determining which solution we will find. For example, gradient descent finds the minimal Euclidean norm solution in linear regression whereas coordinate descent will converge to a sparse solution. I am also interested in the effect of early stopping and parameterization on the solution found by training. Problems that I have been thinking about include the implicit regularization in early-stopped linear models, ReLU regression and matrix factorization.
